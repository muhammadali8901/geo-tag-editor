What is EXIF Metadata and What Does it Reveal?

First created several decades ago to help professional photographers meticulously log mechanical lens settings like aperture, shutter speed, and ISO, EXIF data has ballooned in the modern era to include highly invasive tracking metrics. If left untouched, it documents the precise second and timestamp you took the photograph, the specific device make, model, and software version, and most dangerously, the incredibly accurate GPS latitude, longitude, and altitude altitude.

While this structured metadata is exceptionally useful for automatically grouping and mapping out your private vacation photos inside a secured digital diary, or cataloging a massive real estate portfolio for an agency, it transitions into a monumental risk the moment that photo is posted to a public blog, an unprotected web forum, or shared through raw messaging app chains. If you do not actively engage a tool to remove geotag from image files before distributing them outside your trusted, isolated digital circle, you are effectively attaching an accurate blueprint of your whereabouts to your public profile.
