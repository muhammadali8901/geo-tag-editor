Remove GPS Location from a Photo Before Sharing It

Most photos from smartphones and digital cameras include hidden GPS coordinates. When you share that image, the location can travel with it, even if you only intended to post a picture of a local café, a neighbourhood market, or a shop window.

This online tool is designed to remove the GPS location from a photo securely and privately. The clean image is downloaded back to your device, with the location metadata removed and the visible pixels left untouched.

Why this matters

GPS data inside a photo can reveal more than the scene itself. A single picture still can disclose your home address, the exact location of a local event, or the coordinates of a private business space.

How it works

  1. Upload a JPEG image to the browser tool.
  2. The tool reads the image’s EXIF metadata and finds the GPS section.
  3. It removes only the location data, leaving the rest of the metadata intact.
  4. You download a new image file that looks the same but no longer contains GPS coordinates.

What stays and what goes

The tool removes the GPS block from the image metadata. The photo itself is not re-encoded, so there is no loss of quality. Any visible content, colors, and resolution remain the same.

Best for

  • People sharing photos from home or work.
  • Small businesses publishing product or storefront images.
  • Content creators publishing local stories online.
  • Anyone who wants a fast, privacy-centered way to clean location data.

Local privacy reminder

If the photo was taken near a residence, at a nearby park, or inside a local store, removing the GPS tags is especially important. The coordinate data in these images can give away exact block-level details even when the visible scene is generic.

Use it without risk

This tool processes your photo entirely in the browser. The file is never uploaded to a server, and the cleaned version is returned directly to your device. That makes it a safe choice for privacy-conscious users.

Want to do the opposite?

If you need to add or change a location instead of removing it, the companion tool can write GPS coordinates into an image securely and privately.
